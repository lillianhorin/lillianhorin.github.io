About Me

I'm a Los Angeles-born, Boston-based biologist and visual storyteller. My life's purpose is to bridge the gap between scientific discovery and patient care. In my professional work, I work to advance access to therapies. In my creative work, I'm fascinated by narratives about health, and how storytelling can make scientific knowledge more accessible for patients.

The longer version, for those interested

Biology, medicine, and storytelling have always been inextricably linked for me. When I was growing up, I felt like I lived in the hospital, a place where things can feel out of control. Out of a desire to make sense of it all, I asked so, so many questions. Before I knew it, I was spending my free time drawing organ systems and mechanistic pathways in fountains of color. Understanding how disease & therapeutics worked had captured me like a moth to a light.

I completed my Ph.D. at Harvard Medical School in Tim Mitchison's lab, where I had 7 different projects spanning cancer biology, mechanisms of platelet activation, and alternative medicines. My dissertation focused on the unexpected mechanisms of microtubule-based cancer drugs, using non-dividing cells as a model. I also fell in love with quantitative microscopy, and created a YouTube channel to make the Ph.D. admissions process accessible to anyone who was interested.

After my Ph.D., I felt a profound sense of loss without the microscopes that brought me so much joy - so I picked up a camera. What at first felt like a coping mechanism became a form of self-expression and an exploration of the world around us.﻿

In addition to my work in health and photography, I run, hike with my rescue beagle, and enjoy the beach.
